FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Have questions? We have answers. Here are the most common queries from our investors.

A Systematic Investment Plan (SIP) is a disciplined method of investing a fixed amount in mutual funds at regular intervals — weekly, monthly, or quarterly. SIPs leverage the power of compounding and rupee cost averaging, making them ideal for long-term wealth creation. You can start a SIP with as little as ₹500 per month.

A common guideline is the 50-30-20 rule — invest at least 20% of your monthly income. However, the ideal amount depends on your financial goals, liabilities, and timeline. Our advisors can help you determine the right SIP amount using a personalized goal-based financial plan. Even ₹1,000/month can create significant wealth over 15–20 years.

Mutual funds are regulated by SEBI and are a relatively safe investment vehicle compared to direct stocks. While they are subject to market risks, diversification across multiple securities reduces individual stock risk significantly. Debt funds offer more stability, while equity funds provide higher growth potential. Long-term investing (5+ years) typically mitigates short-term volatility.

The National Pension System (NPS) is a government-backed retirement savings scheme that allows you to invest in a mix of equity, corporate bonds, and government securities. It offers tax benefits up to ₹2 lakh under Section 80C and 80CCD(1B), and your corpus grows tax-free. At retirement, 60% is available as a lump sum while 40% is converted into an annuity for regular pension income.

An Initial Public Offering (IPO) is when a private company first offers its shares to the public. You can apply for IPO shares through your demat account during the subscription window. Allotment is done by lottery if the IPO is oversubscribed. Our experts analyze each IPO's fundamentals, valuation, and market conditions to help you decide whether to apply and at which price band.
